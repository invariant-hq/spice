Ask your caller a question and wait for the answer.

Use this when the task is ambiguous, when a step would be destructive
or hard to reverse, or when the workspace contradicts your brief. Your
turn pauses until the answer arrives as this call's result — so ask
precisely, include the options you see, and say which you would pick
and why.

Do not use this for progress updates: your final message is the
report. One good question beats guessing; asking twice about the same
thing means your brief was too thin — say so in the question.
